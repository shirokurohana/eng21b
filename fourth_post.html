Illness and death in “Time to Come”

May 7, 2023 by nati

Text #3: Author & Movement: Walt Whitman (American, Transcendentalism) Text, Genre & Date: “Time to Come” (Poem, 1842)

“The grave will take me; earth will close O’er cold dull limbs and ashy face; But where, O, Nature, where shall be The soul’s abiding place?” - Walt Whitman

Analysis: In this poem, Walt Whitman writes about the certainty of death that comes to all humans. Not only does he ponder on this subject, but asks questions about where human souls go after death showing to us that he must’ve believed in some afterlife with the soul leaving the body to go elsewhere. He concurs with the rest of humanity of the mystery of death. Since he is from the Transcendentalism period, a time where nature was seen by authors as a source of divine truth, it is easy to understand why he invites nature into a conversation about the lodging of human souls at death including his own.
